Richard Feynman

Professor of Physics at Caltech

Recepient of the Nobel Prize in Physics, for contributions to quantum electrodynamics. Graduate of MIT and Princeton.

Research Interests

  • I'm very interested in Quantum Electrodynamics (QED). Specifically, how the Path Integral Formulation can be used to describe all phenomena involving electrically charged particles as well as give an account of matter and light interaction.
  • I'm studying the superfluidity of supercooled liquid helium. In these cases helium displays an uncharacteristic smoothness to its flow. I'm working on a quantum-mechanical explanation for Dr. Lev D. Landau's theory of superfluidity by using the Schrodinger equation.
  • The weak decay of the neutron into an electron can be studied through a combination of vector an axial currents. I'm collaborating with Murray Gell-Mann on an approach that shows current coupling can be described through vector and axial currents.
